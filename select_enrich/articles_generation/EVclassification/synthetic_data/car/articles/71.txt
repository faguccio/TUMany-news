    <div class="main-content">
        <h2>Tractor Power on Display</h2>
        <p>The annual Tractor Auto Show, a celebration of the enduring power and versatility of tractors, drew enthusiasts from across the country to Springfield, Illinois this weekend. The event, which ran from Friday to Sunday, showcased the latest in agricultural machinery as well as a fascinating lineup of classic models.</p>

        <h2>Not Just for Farmers</h2>
        <p>While tractors are traditionally associated with farming, the Tractor Auto Show aimed to broaden horizons by highlighting their diverse uses in industries such as construction and landscaping. "Tractors are the unsung heroes of modern industry," said John Stevens, the event organizer. "This show is about recognizing their contribution beyond just agriculture."</p>

        <h2>Featured Attractions</h2>
        <p>The event featured live demonstrations of the latest tractor technology, including GPS-guided plowing and automated soil analysis. Attendees were also treated to a thrilling tractor pull competition, where machines competed to drag heavy loads over a set distance.</p>

        <h2>Road Trip for Enthusiasts</h2>
        <p>Many visitors turned the event into a road trip, traveling in caravans to Springfield. Emily and Tom Jenkins, who drove over 300 miles from Kansas City, noted, "The camaraderie among tractor enthusiasts is unmatched. It's a joy to see the innovation in modern tractors while reminiscing about the classics we grew up with."</p>

        <h2>Keeping it Green</h2>
        <p>While the focus was firmly on traditional powertrains, a section of the show dedicated to sustainable practices in farming hinted at a greener future for tractors. Displays included tractors optimized for biodiesel use and models with reduced emissions, demonstrating a commitment to environmental stewardship without veering into the territory of electric vehicles.</p>
